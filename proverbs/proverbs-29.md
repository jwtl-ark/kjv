# Proverbs 29

**1** He, that being often reproved hardeneth his neck, shall suddenly be destroyed, and that without remedy. ^proverbs-29-1

**2** When the righteous are in authority, the people rejoice: but when the wicked beareth rule, the people mourn. ^proverbs-29-2

**3** Whoso loveth wisdom rejoiceth his father: but he that keepeth company with harlots spendeth his substance. ^proverbs-29-3

**4** The king by judgment establisheth the land: but he that receiveth gifts overthroweth it. ^proverbs-29-4

**5** A man that flattereth his neighbour spreadeth a net for his feet. ^proverbs-29-5

**6** In the transgression of an evil man there is a snare: but the righteous doth sing and rejoice. ^proverbs-29-6

**7** The righteous considereth the cause of the poor: but the wicked regardeth not to know it. ^proverbs-29-7

**8** Scornful men bring a city into a snare: but wise men turn away wrath. ^proverbs-29-8

**9** If a wise man contendeth with a foolish man, whether he rage or laugh, there is no rest. ^proverbs-29-9

**10** The bloodthirsty hate the upright: but the just seek his soul. ^proverbs-29-10

**11** A fool uttereth all his mind: but a wise man keepeth it in till afterwards. ^proverbs-29-11

**12** If a ruler hearken to lies, all his servants are wicked. ^proverbs-29-12

**13** The poor and the deceitful man meet together: the LORD lighteneth both their eyes. ^proverbs-29-13

**14** The king that faithfully judgeth the poor, his throne shall be established for ever. ^proverbs-29-14

**15** The rod and reproof give wisdom: but a child left to himself bringeth his mother to shame. ^proverbs-29-15

**16** When the wicked are multiplied, transgression increaseth: but the righteous shall see their fall. ^proverbs-29-16

**17** Correct thy son, and he shall give thee rest; yea, he shall give delight unto thy soul. ^proverbs-29-17

**18** Where there is no vision, the people perish: but he that keepeth the law, happy is he. ^proverbs-29-18

**19** A servant will not be corrected by words: for though he understand he will not answer. ^proverbs-29-19

**20** Seest thou a man that is hasty in his words? there is more hope of a fool than of him. ^proverbs-29-20

**21** He that delicately bringeth up his servant from a child shall have him become his son at the length. ^proverbs-29-21

**22** An angry man stirreth up strife, and a furious man aboundeth in transgression. ^proverbs-29-22

**23** A man’s pride shall bring him low: but honour shall uphold the humble in spirit. ^proverbs-29-23

**24** Whoso is partner with a thief hateth his own soul: he heareth cursing, and bewrayeth it not. ^proverbs-29-24

**25** The fear of man bringeth a snare: but whoso putteth his trust in the LORD shall be safe. ^proverbs-29-25

**26** Many seek the ruler’s favour; but every man’s judgment cometh from the LORD. ^proverbs-29-26

**27** An unjust man is an abomination to the just: and he that is upright in the way is abomination to the wicked. ^proverbs-29-27
